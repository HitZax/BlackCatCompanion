# Sound Effects Placeholders

This folder contains placeholder references for the sound effects used in the Black Cat Companion app. Replace each file with your own audio in MP3 format.

## Sound Files to Replace

### `pet-purr.mp3`

- **Duration**: 0.5 - 1.0 seconds
- **Use**: Plays when the "Pet" button is clicked
- **Suggestion**: Soft purring sound, calming and warm

### `pat-bounce.mp3`

- **Duration**: 0.3 - 0.6 seconds
- **Use**: Plays when the "Pat" button is clicked
- **Suggestion**: Light bouncy sound, playful and uplifting

### `release-shred.mp3`

- **Duration**: 0.8 - 1.2 seconds
- **Use**: Plays when the "Release" button is clicked
- **Suggestion**: Satisfying shredding/ripping sound, like tearing paper

### `reward-celebration.mp3`

- **Duration**: 0.8 - 1.5 seconds
- **Use**: Plays when typing "Done" or "Finish", or when focus session completes
- **Suggestion**: Celebratory sound, success chime or uplifting tone

### `focus-start.mp3`

- **Duration**: 0.4 - 0.8 seconds
- **Use**: Plays when starting a Hyper-Focus session (hold cat's eyes for 3 seconds)
- **Suggestion**: Focused/energizing sound, like a subtle whoosh or bell

### `motivator-chime.mp3`

- **Duration**: 0.3 - 0.7 seconds
- **Use**: Plays when a new motivator/quote is loaded
- **Suggestion**: Light chime or positive notification sound

## How to Add Your Sounds

1. Find or create/download your preferred sound effect audio files (MP3 format recommended)
2. Name them exactly as listed above
3. Place them in this `sounds/` folder
4. Refresh your browser - the app will automatically play the sounds when you interact with it

## Audio Format Recommendations

- **Format**: MP3 (best browser compatibility)
- **Bitrate**: 128 kbps or higher
- **Sample Rate**: 44.1 kHz
- **Channels**: Mono or Stereo

## Tips

- Sounds are set to 60% volume in the app for a non-intrusive experience
- If you don't add audio files, the app will work perfectly fine without them (graceful fallback)
- Test your sounds with different browsers to ensure compatibility
- Consider the cumulative audio experience - multiple sounds should complement each other
